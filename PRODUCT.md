# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Small tuition centre staff, desktop-first: OWNERs and ADMINs doing office/admin work at a front-desk PC (student records, invoicing, payments, scheduling), and TUTORs checking their schedule or marking attendance, sometimes on a tablet or phone between classes. Staff are not necessarily technical — the tool needs to be immediately legible without training, since it's running a real business (enrolments, money, kids' attendance) day to day.

## Product Purpose

A tuition centre management system covering user/role management, class scheduling, student enrolment, attendance tracking, invoicing, and payment recording for Malaysian tuition centres. It replaces spreadsheets/paper for the whole operational loop: enrol a student → schedule their classes → take attendance → generate invoices → record payments. Success looks like an OWNER trusting the dashboard numbers at a glance and staff completing daily tasks (attendance, enrolment, payment recording) without confusion or errors.

## Brand Personality

Warm & approachable, professional & trustworthy. This is admin software, but the subject matter is relationship-driven — parents, kids, tuition fees — so it should never read as cold, sterile, corporate-blue enterprise software. At the same time it's handling money and student records, so it must feel calm, precise, and reliable, not playful or casual. Think: a well-run neighbourhood tuition centre's front desk, not a faceless SaaS back office.

## Anti-references

Avoid the generic cold enterprise SaaS look: sterile gray-and-blue dashboards, dense corporate data-grid feel, faceless B2B tool aesthetics. Avoid anything that makes the human relationships (parent, student, tutor) feel like abstracted database rows. Avoid overly playful/childish treatments too — this is still software adults use to manage money and attendance records; warmth should come through color, tone, and clarity, not decoration.

## Design Principles

1. **Legible over clever.** Staff using this are not necessarily tech-savvy — every screen should be understandable at a glance, with clear labels over icons-only, obvious primary actions, and no hidden functionality.
2. **Warmth through color and tone, not decoration.** The indigo-led palette and plain-language copy should carry the "approachable" feel — not illustrations, mascots, or playful motion.
3. **Trust the numbers.** Dashboard KPIs, invoice totals, and attendance stats are the product's core value — they must always be accurate, clearly sourced, and free of ambiguous states (loading, stale, error) that could shake an OWNER's confidence in the data.
4. **One consistent pattern per problem.** Tables, badges, empty states, modals, and forms should each have exactly one established pattern reused everywhere, not a different treatment per module — consistency reduces cognitive load for non-technical staff.
5. **Desktop-first, tablet-tolerant.** Optimize primarily for a front-desk PC, but keep attendance-taking and schedule-viewing usable on a tutor's tablet/phone between classes.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No specific WCAG level mandated. Baseline good practice: solid text contrast (body text ≥4.5:1), readable text sizes, keyboard-navigable forms, and clear focus states throughout. Standard `htmlspecialchars()` output escaping is already in place; extend the same care to focus/contrast as new UI is built.
